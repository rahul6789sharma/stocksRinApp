Bull Call Spread

As the name suggests, a bull call spread is a bullish strategy. A bull call spread is an options strategy that consists of buying a call option of lower strike price and also selling a call option at a higher strike price. Both options must be in the same expiration cycle.

When to Use this Strategy The strategy comes handy when you have a moderately bullish view on the stock/index.

Strategy Construction
Buy 1 Lower Strike Call Option (leg 1)
Sell 1 OTM Call Option (leg 1)

Important Points about Strategy

  1. Each leg involves the same number of options
  2. Belong to the same expiry series
  3. All strikes prices belong to the same stock/index
  4. This is net debit strategy
  5. This strategy has limited risk and limited profit potential

E.g Nifty

Lower Strike (ITM, Long) 8500
Higher Strike (ATM, short) 8600
Lower Strike Premium Paid 150
Higher Strike Premium Received 120
  • Spread : Higher strike – Lower strike = 8600 – 8500 = 100
  • Net Debit : Premium Paid for lower strike – Premium Received for higher strike =150 – 120 = 30 (per lot)
  • Max Profit Potential : Spread – Net Debit = 100 – 30 = 80 (per lot)
  • Max Loss Potential : Net Debit 30 (per lot)
  • BreakEven Point : Lower Strike + Net Debit =8500 + 30 = 8530
